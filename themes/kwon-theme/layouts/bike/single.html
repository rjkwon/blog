Body parts

Whoops, I mean bike parts. Here's a list of current components. Not listed below, but definitely on my actual bike: chain (whatever the local bike shop stocks), brake (Shimano caliper brake, front wheel only), spokes (whatever the local bike shop stocks, in a radial lacing pattern), tires (Continental Gatorskins), pedal straps (Hold Fast).

Component Make & model Weight Key specs Kwon notes
Saddle Cinelli Unicanitor 296 g Plastic, 7 mm rails This saddle is made of hard plastic (although some of the marketing materials call it "calibrated technopolymer" lol). It is quite rigid, but on the flip side it's very robust when left outside in NYC weather, and it's not as uncomfortable as one might assume.
Seatpost collar Salsa Lip Lock 34 g 31.8mm, aluminum, orange Isn't the name "lip lock" cute?
Seatpost Civia Microadjust 330 g 27.2 mm, aluminum, silver No complaints about this seatpost, although I don't think they make this model anymore
Rear wheel rim Velocity B43 722 g 700c, 43 mm, nonmachined sidewall Rear wheel was originally an Aerospoke, but I liked it so much that I put it on my everyday bike (the one I was using while building this one up), and that bike ended up getting stolen :(
Rear hub Velocity track hub 337 g 32h, silver Durable, never gave me any issues.
Cog All-City Track Cog 262 g 16T x 1/8", stainless steel With the 46T chainring my gear ratio is 2.88 (=46T/16T), which works well for (mostly) flat city streets without killing me on the bridges
Front wheel rim Velocity Deep V 580 g 700c, machined sidewall These were super trendy in Brooklyn around 2010. People would leave the stickers on the rims, like how people would leave the stickers on their New Era caps in the '90s.
Front hub Phil Wood front high flange hub 262 g 32h, silver I always wanted a Phil Wood hub because they seemed super high end (read: expensive). To be honest, I can't really tell the difference between this one and the Velocity track hub which was half the price. Marketing!
Cranks All-City 612 Track Cranks + Chainring 726 g 165 mm, white Pretty solid crankset. I remember I initially got it in silver but then exchanged for the white because I liked the look better.
Pedals Gusset Slim Jim 640 g White, sealed bearing I love these platform pedals. I used to have them in mint green on my old bike that was stolen, and the manufacturer no longer makes them in that color.
Bottom bracket Shimano UN55 286 g English, 68 x 113 mm, square taper Never gives me a problem. I've switched it out exactly once in 13 years.
Frame Nada Bike 1,900 g 52 cm steel (chromoly) frame, horizontal dropouts Nada Bike was a project where you could get a simple single speed steel bike frame for $150 and build up your own bicycle.
Headset FSA Orbit X 95 g 1" threadless, silver I never knew what a headset was (or that it even existed) until I was building this bike.
Handlebars Origin8 Bullhorn Handlebar 320 g 31.8 x 400mm, silver Rode with flat bars chopped down to be comically narrow for a while, which is what is depicted in this image, but I currently ride with the bullhorns which I find lot more comfortable for switching hand positions (and let's be real, I'm not squeezing between cars in rush hour traffic in my 40s anymore)
Stem Thomson Elite X2 150 g 31.8 mm, silver, +-10 degree x 70 mm I love this stem. I also felt like this was super high end when I bought it. (The drawing is not to scale; my actual stem is shorter.)
Fork Soma Straight Blade 890 g 1 1/8" threadless, 300 mm steerer I like the look of a straight blade fork. It's probably more practical to have some offset but I'm not too serious about it tho.
